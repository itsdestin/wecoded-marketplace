How Theming Works

Themes change how DestinCode looks without changing how it works. Think of it like redecorating a room — same furniture layout, completely different vibe.

To create a theme, you just run /theme-builder and describe the vibe you want in everyday language. For example:

"cozy rainy night with warm lighting"
"Hello Kitty pink and cute"
"cyberpunk neon with glowing edges"
"Minecraft-inspired"

Claude will show you concept previews, let you pick your favorite, refine it, and then generate the complete theme. You don't need to understand any of the technical details below to use it. This guide is here so you know what's possible to ask for.

Colors & Font
15 colors that paint every surface and text + optional font
Layout Choices
Bubble shapes, input bar style, header visibility

Wallpaper, gradient, frosted glass effect, patterns
Visual Effects
Floating particles, vignette, film grain, scan lines
Custom Art
Themed icons, mascot accessories, cursors, scrollbars

Each layer is optional. A theme can be as simple as "just colors" or go all-in with every feature.

Colors

Colors are the foundation of every theme. There are 15 color slots that paint every surface, text element, border, and scrollbar in the app. Think of it like a paint palette — each color has a specific job.

✨
Claude picks all 15 colors based on your vibe description. You'll see them in the concept preview and can ask for adjustments ("make the accent warmer" or "lighter background").
  • 4 Surface Colors Canvas (main background), Panel (header/input/status bars), Inset (Claude's bubbles, cards), and Well (deepest recessed areas). These create the depth and layering of the app.
  • Accent Color The personality of your theme. This colors your chat bubbles, buttons, and anything interactive. Plus an on-accent color (text on top of it) that's auto-picked to be readable.
  • Aa
    5 Text Colors A range from bold to barely-visible: primary, secondary, dimmed, muted, and faint. This lets the app create visual hierarchy — important text stands out, hints fade into the background.
  • 2 Border Colors + 2 Scrollbar Colors Lines between sections (full and dimmed), and the scrollbar handle (normal and hovered).

Backgrounds & Wallpapers

The background is what sits behind everything in the app. Some themes use a simple flat color, others use a full image or a color gradient. You can also layer a subtle repeating pattern on top for texture.

✨
Claude picks the best background type for your vibe. A "cozy fireplace" theme might get a warm gradient, while a "Studio Ghibli" theme would get a scenic wallpaper image. You can always request changes.

Additional options for any background type:

  • Opacity — How strong the background is (0% = invisible, 100% = full strength)
  • Pattern overlay — A small image that tiles/repeats across the background for texture (kept very faint)
  • Frosted glass — Makes panels see-through with a blur effect (see next section)
Claude can also create custom combinations — for example, a gradient with a pattern overlay on top, or a wallpaper at half opacity so it's subtle rather than bold. Just describe what you want.

Frosted Glass

When a theme has a wallpaper or gradient background, the panels (header, input bar, bubbles) can be made semi-transparent with a blur — like looking through frosted glass. The background peeks through, creating a layered, atmospheric look.

✨
Claude enables this automatically when it makes sense — usually when there's a wallpaper. You can ask for "more frosted" or "less transparent" to fine-tune it.

Two knobs to turn: blur amount (how frosted the glass is) and transparency (how much background shows through). More blur + more transparency = more dramatic effect.

Claude can set these to any value, not just the four shown above. You can ask for very specific looks like "just barely frosted" or "almost fully transparent with heavy blur."

Chat Bubble Shapes

The shape of message bubbles sets the personality of the whole chat. There are 4 preset shapes to start from, and each one can be further customized: bubbles can be filled (solid background color), transparent (see-through), bordered (outline), or any combination. When a bubble is both filled and bordered, the fill color and border color can be different.

✨
Claude picks the bubble style that fits the mood of your theme. Cute themes tend to get pills, developer themes get flat, and so on. You can always ask for a specific shape or tell Claude to mix things up.

Header & Status Bar

The header is the bar at the very top of the app (shows "DestinCode" and the settings icon). The status bar is the thin strip at the very bottom (shows the current theme name and status). Both can be styled differently or hidden entirely.

✨
Claude picks header and status bar styles that complement the rest of your theme. Immersive themes might hide the header; minimal themes might make the status bar nearly invisible. You can ask for any combination.

Floating Chrome

"Chrome" means all the interface bars together — header, input bar, and status bar. Normally they touch the edges of the window. In floating mode, they all detach and become rounded cards that hover over the content with shadows underneath. It's a more spacious, modern look.

✨
Claude enables floating chrome when the theme has a strong visual background that benefits from the extra breathing room. Most themes use the default. You can request it specifically.

How the two themes handle chrome:

Golden Sunbreak: Default chrome (bars touch the edges), but the input bar is individually set to floating. So the input floats while the header and status don't.
Kitty Pink Classic: Default chrome. Everything is flush and clean. Fits the simple, tidy aesthetic.

Corner Roundness

Every surface in the app has corners — bubbles, buttons, panels, cards. A theme controls how rounded or sharp those corners are across the entire app at once. This alone can make the same app feel sleek and techy (sharp corners) or soft and friendly (round corners).

✨
Claude picks corner roundness to match the vibe. Cute themes get rounder corners, tech themes get sharper ones. There are 7 levels from nearly square to fully circular, and Claude can set each level independently.

How the two themes use corners:

Golden Sunbreak: Generously rounded (8px to 36px). Everything feels smooth and organic, matching the warm dreamy aesthetic. Nothing has a sharp edge.
Kitty Pink Classic: Also very rounded (7px to 36px). Combined with the pill bubble shape, everything has a soft, toylike quality. Not a sharp corner in sight.

Fonts

A theme can override the font for the entire app. This is great for themes with strong character — a retro pixel font for an arcade theme, a handwritten style for a cozy journal theme, or an elegant serif for something formal.

✨
Claude selects a font when it fits the vibe. Most themes use the default system font, which looks clean on every device. Fonts are loaded automatically from Google Fonts — you don't need to install anything.

How the two themes handle fonts:

Golden Sunbreak: Default system font. The vibe comes from colors and effects, not typography.
Kitty Pink Classic: Default system font. The cuteness comes from the shapes and colors instead.

Visual Effects

These are atmospheric overlays that float on top of the app. They don't affect how you use it — they're purely vibes. Think of them like lighting and ambiance in a room. There are four types: floating particles, darkened edges (vignette), film grain texture, and retro scan lines.

✨
Claude adds effects that match the atmosphere. A cozy night theme might get slow-floating dust and a vignette. A cyberpunk theme might get scan lines and neon particles. A clean cute theme usually gets no effects at all. Each effect's intensity is carefully tuned so it enhances rather than distracts.

How the two themes use effects:

Claude can combine effects and tune them precisely. You can ask for things like "very subtle snow, barely noticeable" or "heavy scan lines like an old arcade machine." Claude can also create custom particle shapes that match your theme — like cherry blossoms, lightning bolts, or musical notes.

Custom Art & Icons

Themes can replace the default icons in the app and add themed accessories to the DestinCode mascot character. There are 6 icon slots, 3 mascot expressions, and a few bonus slots for cursor and scrollbar art. All of these are optional — many themes don't use any custom art.

✨
Claude creates all art assets automatically when they fit the vibe. Brand/IP themes (like "Star Wars" or "Minecraft") get the most custom art. Abstract vibe themes often skip custom art and focus on colors and effects instead.

How the two themes use custom art:

Golden Sunbreak: Goes all-in on art.
• Custom send button icon
• All 3 mascot expressions with themed accessories
• Gold-tinted scrollbar
• Default cursor (no need for a themed one)
Kitty Pink Classic: No custom art at all.
• All default icons
• No mascot customization
• Default cursor and scrollbar
The cuteness is all in the colors and shapes.
